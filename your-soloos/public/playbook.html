Sales Playbook

Your offer,in one place.

This is a starter template. Replace the prompts below with your own positioning, and your team will have a shared reference for every conversation.

Positioning

What we do, in one sentence

The clearest version of who you help and the change you create.

We help [who] achieve [outcome] by [how].

Tip: lead with the outcome your buyer cares about, not your process. The process is how you deliver it, not why they buy.
Who it's for

Our ideal customer

The people you serve best, and the signals that tell you a lead is a strong fit.

Best fit

Describe the organization, size, stage, and situation where you do your best work.

Trigger events

What's usually happening in their world right before they reach out or say yes.

Not a fit

The traits that make a project hard to deliver well. Knowing these saves everyone time.

Where we meet them

The channels, referrals, and communities that bring in the right leads.

The problem

The problem we solve

Name the pain in your buyer's own words. The sharper this is, the easier everything else gets.

  • What does the status quo cost them, in time, money, or risk?
  • What have they already tried that didn't work, and why?
  • What's the "expensive alternative" you're a better answer to?

Discovery

Questions that move a deal forward

A short list you can return to on any call. Listen more than you pitch.

  • What made you start looking into this now?
  • What does success look like six months after we work together?
  • Who else is involved in the decision, and what matters to them?
  • What happens if you do nothing?

Pricing

How we talk about price

Your pricing logic and the simple way you frame it on a call.

Anchor on the value and outcome first. State the price plainly, then go quiet and let them respond.

Tip: have a clear, repeatable structure so price never feels improvised.
Next steps

Closing and follow-through

Make the next step obvious and easy at the end of every conversation.

  • Confirm the single next action and who owns it.
  • Send a short recap the same day while it's fresh.
  • Set a real date for the follow-up. "I'll be in touch" is not a date.
